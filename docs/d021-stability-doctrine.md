# D-021 — STABILITY DOCTRINE: EVERY FIRE BURNS OUT, EVERYONE CAN MOVE
### Director's design review of D-018. New decision record (exempt class per S8 §4); elaborates D-018 without amending its frozen frame. Lands at M2/M5/M8 as scheduled below.

---

## PART 1 — THE DOCTRINE (project-wide, all systems)

**The paired-feedback rule.** Every positive feedback loop in the design must ship with at least one negative feedback loop that *strengthens with amplitude*. Grievance→riot→economic damage→grievance is legal only because riot participation itself generates exhaustion, discharge, and exit at rates that grow with the outburst. This rule is project-wide: it binds unrest, markets (price rises pull supply), epidemics (susceptibles deplete), and war (exhaustion) equally.

**No absorbing doom states — as a CI test, not a wish.** New calibration-battery entries:
- **Ignite-and-burn-out:** across seeds, autoplay with a *deliberately negligent* AI (needs left unmet) must show unrest episodes that ignite AND terminate endogenously; grievance and unrest-activity time series must show no permanent saturation lock across any 200-turn window.
- **No empty menus:** the D-019 verb-freeze matrix must guarantee ≥1 remediation option available in every legitimacy-crisis state, under every regime, always — the director's original D-010 promise ("a few options should be given") made structural.
- **Collapse is a transition, not a trap:** forced-collapse runs must resolve into functioning successor configurations (successor states, warlord regions, village autarky) that themselves pass the ignite-and-burn-out test. "Beyond repair" is definitionally impossible: repair may take a century; an unplayable black hole may never exist.

A doom loop discovered by these tests is a mechanism bug — fix the mechanism, never script the outcome (frozen calibration rule).

## PART 2 — THE RELEASE VALVES (why outbursts end even when needs stay unmet)

Organizing frame: grievance expresses through three competing channels — **Exit, Voice, or Endurance** — and the sim always keeps at least one channel open.

1. **Expression discharges pressure.** The outburst itself vents the stock: a riot converts accumulated grievance into expressed grievance ("the message was sent"), dropping G even when nothing was fixed. Riots are literally pressure-release events.
2. **Fatigue.** Participation costs wages, safety, and energy; unrest activity accrues per-bucket fatigue that suppresses further participation and decays only with quiet. Most riots last days; the math should too.
3. **Exit.** Angry people leave before they revolt twice: out-migration to frontier, other settlements, or abroad drains the aggrieved bucket along the real network. Openness of exits (free movement, available land, transport reach) governs the valve — and closing exits is a *policy choice that redirects pressure into Voice*, with everything that implies.
4. **Endurance.** Faith access, community institutions, and habituation absorb grievance into acquiescence — consolation as a modeled mechanism. Effective, never infinite.
5. **Organization decay.** Escalation beyond riot requires movements: leadership (notables), cohesion, funding. Cohesion decays without wins; leaders die, defect, are bought, or fall out. Uprisings that stall, fracture.
6. **The state acts by default.** Player inaction is not world inaction: governors, garrisons, and local elites respond with their own competence per the delegation doctrine — order gets restored badly rather than not at all.
7. **The economy self-heals.** Shortage raises prices, prices pull supply and substitution (frozen solver behavior); informal economies and charity institutions degrade-but-satisfy needs the formal economy misses.
8. **Generational decay.** Grievance stocks decay faster across cohort replacement — children inherit a fraction of their parents' grudges. Memory is long (frozen: grudges outlive causes) but not immortal.

## PART 3 — MOBILITY: RULED ALREADY IN-FRAME, ELABORATED

D-018 §5 already commits mobility as conserved rate-based flows (a few % per sim-year, `TUNE`) driven by destination demand and access. The director's requirements map onto it as follows — no amendment needed:

- **Promotion flows + their policy hooks:** Peasant→Laborer (urban labor demand; land policy), Laborer→Artisan (training institutions, guild openness), →Intelligentsia (education access — *the* lever: school institutions raise the flow and feed the Prospects need), Merchant→Capitalist (property rights, capital markets), →Clergy/Soldiers/Bureaucrats (institutional recruitment budgets).
- **Demotion flows (new elaboration):** Merchant→Laborer via bankruptcy (panics, war); Artisan→Laborer via automation displacement (already their anger trigger — now also a flow); Peasant→landless Laborer via land loss/enclosure policies; Aristocracy shrinks via confiscation, purge, or revolution; Enslaved↔free strictly via manumission/abolition/conquest institutions.
- **"Farmers unionize and empower" is organization, not class change:** unionization raises a bucket's *organization level* within its class (movement framework), which multiplies political weight and strike capacity. Legality policies (ban/tolerate/recognize unions) move it — banning lowers organization but raises grievance. Empowerment = organization × enfranchisement, both policy-touchable.
- **Intra- and inter-settlement job movement:** settlement labor markets bid for workers; wage differentials pull migration along the real network within catchments and between cities — the D-009 world model doing labor's version of trade.
- **Education is a flow with a lag:** literacy investments change bucket composition over sim-decades and — via rising expectations (frozen) — raise liberty/prospects salience. Educating your population remains a deliberate gamble; that tension is kept, not patched.

## PART 4 — LANDING SCHEDULE

- **M2:** grievance/needs land with decay + generational-decay parameters in the data files.
- **M5 (unrest-lite):** ships valves 1, 2, 3, 6, 7 *with* the unrest it ships — the brakes install with the gas pedal, never after. Ignite-and-burn-out test enters the battery at M5.
- **M8 (full ladder):** valves 4, 5, 8; movements/organization; D-019 matrix with the no-empty-menu guarantee; collapse-as-transition test.
- Mobility flows: M2 (demographic flows) → M5 (policy hooks) → M8 (organization/enfranchisement).

## PART 5 — ANNO COMPARISON, RULED (for the record)

Supply side: yes — needs are satisfied by real goods baskets flowing through real logistics, Anno-like by design. Response side: deliberately not Anno. Anno's citizens are memoryless (grievance resets when schnapps arrives), promotion is a player-clicked upgrade, and unhappiness caps at production pauses. Here people remember (habituation, grudges), move on their own (flows), and escalate into politics — which is exactly why this doctrine exists: Anno avoids doom loops by deleting memory; we keep memory and pair it with history's real release valves.
